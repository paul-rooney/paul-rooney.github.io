Building an email newsletter or having computers do it for me

So—here’s the thing: having to put together an email newsletter should be straightforward. It should be quick. It should probably even be fun. But when you have the sales team making promises to their advertising clients, you end up with a whole mess of content. Something like thirty-plus ads. Which has to be balanced out with actual content that isn’t just ads. So add another twenty articles for good measure. Then, the directors want to know if we can just move this one, tiny, titchy, thing around, or maybe add in a big image here to push something else.

This isn’t so straightforward. Or quick. And definitely not fun.

So I decided to try to automate this process—at least as far as is possible using our office’s choice of email service provider, Campaign Monitor.
